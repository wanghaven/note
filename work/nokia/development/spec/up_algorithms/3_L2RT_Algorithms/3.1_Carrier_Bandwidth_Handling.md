### 3.1.1.0-1 Carrier Resource Determination of the number of PRBs for the DL and UL carrier bandwidth (ID: 11110609)

**3.1.1.0-1.0-3**  (ID: `12820021`)

Carrier Resource The number of PRBs for the UL carrier depends on uplink bandwidth (NRCELL/NRCELL_FDD/chBwUl or NRCELL/chBw[CB015113-A]for symmetric UL bandwidth, or NRCELL/chBwUlTdd for asymmetric UL bandwidth)[End CB015113-A]and deployment (NRCELL/cellTechnology and NRCELL/nrarfcn).

The number of PRBs for the DL carrier depends on downlink bandwidth (NRCELL/NRCELL_FDD/chBwDl or NRCELL/chBw) and deployment (NRCELL/cellTechnology and NRCELL/nrarfcn).

Note that in TDD deployment, UL carrier bandwidth is identical to the DL carrier bandwidth [CB015113-A]for symmetric UL bandwidth; UL carrier bandwidth is unequal to the DL carrier bandwidth for asymmetric UL bandwidth[End CB015113-A].

For the supported values of carrier bandwidth:

  * refer to 5G_UP_ALG_1800_replaced([11110650](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kpGwJfvSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)) for TDD and FR2 bands

#### 3.1.2.2.0-1 UE and 5G-L2-PS Active BWP Active BWP during the RA procedure (ID: 11110853)

**3.1.2.2.0-1.0-2**  (ID: `13419905`)

UE and 5G-L2-PS Active BWP For an UE, any UL or DL scheduling always occur in the UE Active BWP, irrespective of the nature of the transmitted information.

The general applying rule is that, in order to be able to receive a certain type of information, discriminated by the RNTI the DCI is scrambled with, UE must be configured with a searchSpace in the active BWP associated to that RNTI type, otherwise UE shall not monitor PDCCH for the corresponding RNTI.

In particular, in order to be able to perform RA procedure in a given BWP, UE must be configured with _ra-searchSpace for that BWP, otherwise RA-RNTI and TC-RNTI are not expected._

The active BWP during the RA procedure shall be understood as follows according to the cell configuration:

1\. Cell NSA only:

In NSA, UE is always already configured directly into the dedicated BWP before the first attempt to connect to the cell. Therefore, UE is not requested to perform BWP switch via DCI. Consequently, for an NSA UE, the UE active BWP shall always be the dedicated BWP and all the UL/DL transmissions addressed to an NSA UE should have the corresponding search space configured in the dedicated BWP, including the _ra-searchSpace. Consequently:_

\- UE shall perform the CFRA procedure in the Dedicated BWP.

\- UE shall perform the CBRA procedure for the SgNB Addition in the dedicated BWP (1-1)

\- UE shall perform the CBRA procedure for a 5G connected state RACH in the dedicated BWP (1-2)

\- ~~Note: 5G_UP_ALG_1826_replaced([11110973](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kpJMZ_vSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)) specify the BWP where the scheduling is performed for an NSA UE. The allocation should be understood as frequency domain resource allocation area only and not as BWP where the scheduling is performed, even though it is possible that the allocation to be restricted to the position and the size of the Initial BWP or CORESET#0, That is, the grant is always sent in the active BWP which, as explained above, in NSA, is always matching the dedicated BW and UE shall be able to receive it only if it has an searchSpace to listen for it.~~

For the DL C[B,F]RA procedure part (RA-Msg2), the Frequency Domain allocation for a DCI Format 1_0 in a common search space (Type1 for the RA procedure) is restricted by the CORESET(0) size starting at the first RB of the CORESET linked to the Type1 PDCCH configured in the UE active BWP (a dedicated one).

For the UL CBRA procedure part (RA-Msg3, initial transmission and retransmissions), the Frequency Domain allocation allowed for the RAR (initial Tx) & DCI Format 0_0 (reTx) is restricted to the intersection of all BWPs configured with a PRACH and the initial BWP (even if the initial is not configured with a RACH because the UE active BWP in NSA call is never the initial BWP and it is not able to fallback itself in the initial BWP. Until the contention resolution with an additional UL grant, the UE uses the TC-RNTI configured by the RA-Msg2, leading to have a specific Resource Indication Value (RIV) encoding rule in the FDRA in the RAR or in DCI Format 0_0 (reTx).

For the UL CFRA procedure part, the Frequency Domain allocation in RAR is restricted by the size of the FDRA field (14 bits) and the intersection of the initial BWP and the UE active BWP, as for RAR in case of CBRA. For the retransmission, the Frequency Domain allocation for DCI Format 0_0 (reTx) is restricted respectively by the size of the initial BWP for the DCI format 0_0 within the UE active BWP. In CFRA procedure, the RA procedure is completed at the reception of the RA-Msg2 in the UE, therefore the RA-Msg3 (initial Tx and reTx) are regular UL transmissions restricted by the FDRA of the RAR (14 bits) or DCI Format 0_0 (nBits according to the size of the initial BWP) in the UE active BWP.

The RA procedure, including the RA-Msg3 Frequency Domain allocation, is detailed in the requirement: [11152251: Random Access Procedures (CBRA/CFRA)](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kse0SfvSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ?oslc_config.context=https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/gc/configuration/9).

2\. Cell SA only:

Moreover, UE is configured to directly connect into the dedicated BWP. Consequently, a CFRA is always considered in the dedicated BWP._

o If the CBRA procedure corresponds to a HO case in SA mode, UE is already configured with dedicated configuration that should have configured the associated _ra-searchSpace in the dedicated BWP. o If the CBRA procedure corresponds to a RACH in connected mode, UE is already configured with dedicated configuration that should have configured the associated _ra-searchSpace in the dedicated BWP. Consequently, UE is considered in the dedicated BWP (2-3)._

\- In a CFRA procedure, the preamble used in the CFRA procedure by the UE was previously configured in it through a the RRC reconfiguration procedure before the preamble transmission(s). The UE is already configured with dedicated configuration, including the _ra-searchSpace in the dedicated BWP. Moreover, UE is configured to be directly active into the dedicated BWP. Consequently, a CFRA is always considered in the UE active dedicated BWP, known at the U-Plane user setup procedure (Preamble is associated to a C-RNTI by the C-Plane and at the RA-Msg1 the U-Plane knowns the first active BWP). The RIV encoding has the same constraints as in NSA case (see above)._

\- In a CBRA procedure, determined by the preamble used in the RA-Msg1, the UE and its state are unknown in the gNB before the reception of the RA-Msg3 which contains enough information to identify what is the UE active BWP (see below). Therefore, the U-Plane allocates in the RA-Msg2 a TC-RNTI and configures a PUSCH transmission in order to receive the RA-Msg3 with additional information allowing to determine the UE active BWP and the state of the UE.

The UE configured with a TC-RNTI applies a specific procedure to compute the location of the expected PUSCH transmission for the RA-Msg3 by adding on the start RB sent in the FDRA (RAR, DCI 0_0) the difference between the start RB of the initial BWP and its active BWP. Nevertheless, the UE has only one active BWP at a time and all channels received/transmitted are within the UE active BWP, leading to restrict the RA-Msg3 in the intersection of the UE active BWP and the initial BWP. As the UE is not known before the reception of the RA-Msg3, the gNB must determine the whole possible UE active BWP in order to allocate the set of PUSCH resources in the common area of the BWPs configured with a RACH. In the gNB as long as only one PRACH per cell is configured, the gNB is able to add, on top of the initial BWP, all other BWPs which fully overlap the PRACH.

In the RA-Msg3, the UE has only 2 options:

o The UE sends a RRC message through an UL-CCCH.

The UL-CCCH can be a RRC setup req or a RRC reestablishment request or a RRC resume[1] request (other UL-CCCH messages are not supported).

In that case the UE active BWP is the initial BWP.

o The UE sends a MAC Control Element CRNTI to indicate its current C-RNTI (procedures triggered by the UE (Beam Failure Recovery, UL grant request without SR configured...), or by the gNB (RRC reconfiguration with UE synchronization...)).

If the C-RNTI reported in the MAC CE is unknown, the gNB will not close the contention (and the UE may retry the RA procedure...). Otherwise, the C-RNTI is known and the UE-Plane determines the current UE active BWP according the last UE active bandwidth part before the reception of the RA-Msg3, and whether this BWP overlaps the PRACH in the cell or not:

\- If the previous BWP (saved for the C-RNTI context) includes the PRACH of the cell, the current UE active BWP is the same as the previous one.

\- If the previous BWP does not include the PRACH of the cell, the UE switches itself in the initial BWP as its previous BWP did not contain any RACH configured.

The RA procedure, including the RA-Msg3 Frequency Domain allocation, is detailed in the requirement: [11152251: Random Access Procedures (CBRA/CFRA)](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kse0SfvSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ?oslc_config.context=https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/gc/configuration/9). [End PR876217-26R3]

3\. Cell NSA+SA:

[Before PR876217-26R3] ; In NSA+SA case, the active BWP is derived from the above specifications, depending whether the UE is connected in NSA or in SA mode. 

\- Active BWP ambiguity during the RA procedure in NSA+SA mode:

Consequently, L2-PS shall consider that CBRA corresponding to the cases (1-1),(1-2) ,(2-2),(2-3) is performed the Initial BWP. This statement shall be revisited when L2-PS is enhanced such that to be able to discriminate between the mentioned cases. [End PR876217-26R3]

[PR876217-26R3] ; In SA + NSA as for previous cases, the UE active BWP is still determined before the RA-Msg3 (CFRA) or after the reception of the RA-Msg3 for CBRA procedure, according to the above specifications. The UE active BWP ambiguity for the RA-Msg3 allocation is removed by allocating the resources only in the common area between the initial and all BWPs fully overlapping the PRACH of the cell. [End PR876217-26R3].

In SA call setup procedure and until the RRC reconfiguration procedure adding more than one dedicated BWP in the UE, the UE active BWP after the reception of the RA-Msg3 is managed by the RRC messages sent to the UE and according the NIDD parameters (NRCELL/ueConnectionEstablishmentMode, NRCELL/actSmallestRrcSetup and the PDCCH Configuration of the initial BWP) which control the procedure until the first DRB setup:

· When the RA-Msg3 contains an UL-CCCH (RRC Setup Request, RRC Reestablishment Request or RRC Resume Request[1]) the UE active BWP is the initial BWP. As soon as the contention resolution is done by the MAC Control Element CRI and until the release of the C-RNTI by the UE, the UE active BWP can be modified only by a RRC message indicating the first active BWP ID corresponding to The BWP configured with dedicated parameters. To allow DCI BWP switch, the UE must be configured with more than 1 BWP with dedicated parameters.

· When the gNB triggers a RRC setup procedure, the NRCELL/ueConnectionEstablishmentMode defined the first BWP configured as a dedicated until the RRC reconfiguration triggered to bring up the first DRB.

If the parameter is set to "useInitialBwp", the gNB configures the UE with the initial BWP as a dedicated BWP in the UE (SRS, dedicated PUCCH, SR, CSI-RS for tracking, CSI-RS/IM...) and indicate to the UE that the first active BWP is its BWP-0 (the initial with more capability that the one defined in the SIB1) until the first DRB setup procedure.

If the parameter is set to "useDedicatedBwp", the gNB configures the UE with the smallest BWP as the first dedicated BWP in the UE (SRS, dedicated PUCCH, SR, CSI-RS for tracking, CSI-RS/IM...) and indicate to the UE that the first active BWP is its BWP-1 until the first DRB setup procedure.

The NRCELL/actSmallestRrcSetup impacts only the RRC procedure to reach the configuration first dedicated BWP defined by the "ueConnectionEstablishmentMode" parameter:

\- If the parameter is set to true, the RRC setup message (DL-CCCH[RLC-TM]) contains the minimum set of parameter to bring up the SRB1 on the initial BWP (24 Bytes) allowing to activate the RLC segmentation (RLC-AM mode) for the next RRC procedures and especially, the RRC reconfiguration procedure allowing to configure and activate the first dedicated BWP. The RLC segmentation allows to improve the coverage by using the NRCELL/dlLaIniMcs and then split the large RRC message in several transmissions using DCI Format 1_0. 

\- If the parameter is set to false, the RRC setup message (DL-CCCH[RLC-TM]) contains the whole set of parameter to configure the first BWP as a dedicated one. As the DCI Format 1_0 can use in Frequency Domain at most the number of RB defined by the CORESET(0) size, the amount of data to send does not allow to use the dlLaIniMcs. Therefore the gNB has to degrade the coverage of the RRC Setup by increasing the MCS according to the size of the RRC setup and the size of the CORESET(0). In the worst case, the coverage degradation is much larger than 3 dB. 

· When the gNB triggers a RRC reestablishment procedure, the UE is on the initial BWP with the SRB1 and at the reception of RRC reestablishmment complete, the UE is exactly in the same state as with the small RRC setup. Then, the gNB triggers a RRC reconfiguration procedure to fully reconfigure the UE with the same set of parameters the UE had just before the reestablishment procedure. The gNB uses the RLC segmentation capability of the SRB1. At the end of the RRC reconfiguration procedure, the UE is active on the dedicated BWP (BWP-1 in the UE).

· When the gNB triggers a RRC resume procedure, the UE is on the initial BWP with the SRB1 (same state as after the small RRC setup or reestablishment procedures). According to the content of the UL-CCCH, the gNB can restore the UE in active state by sending, in the in the RRC Resume message, the full RRC configuration the UE had when it was switched to inactive state. When the UE is set in active state, the dedicated BWP is the dedicated BWP (BWP-1 in the UE). Other options are allowed according to the context like to set back the UE in the inactive state.

The handover procedures (SA or NSA) use the dedicated BWP as the first active BWP on the target cell. The RRC reconfiguration done on the source cell requests the UE to activate the dedicated BWP before the RA-Msg1 on the target cell.

When the UE has to decode the SI or Paging, it remains in its active BWP. The DCI Format 1_0 in the common search space is agnostic to the UE active BWP as long as the UE is configured to receive them in its BWP(s).

As soon as the UE has at least 2 dedicated BWP configured, the DCI BWP switch is allowed when the DCI Format [0,1]_1 is configured in all dedicated BWPs configured in the UE and when the criteria to trigger the switch are met.

The RRC BWP switch is used when the UE is active on the initial BWP and:

· When the the gNB configures one or several dedicated BWP which are not the initial BWP (ex call setup above).

· When receiving a RA-Msg1, if the last UE active BWP doesn't have a RACH configuration, the UE switches implicitly to the initial BWP (as no RACH is configured for its active BWP) and the gNB needs to switch back the UE to the dedicated BWP using the RRC BWP switch procedure ([11111051: RRC BWP switch procedure](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kpJzevvSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ?oslc_config.context=https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/gc/configuration/9)) because the initial BWP is not configured anymore as a dedicated BWP (and no DCI Format [0,1]_1).
