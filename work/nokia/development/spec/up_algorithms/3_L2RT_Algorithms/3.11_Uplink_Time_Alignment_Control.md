### 3.11.3.0-1 Computation of Timing Advance Command Long-Term TO Computation for TAC (ID: 11150211)

**3.11.3.0-1.0-3**  (ID: `13470309`)

Computation of Timing Advance Command 13470309: Computation of Timing Advance Command The long term timing power offset parameter represents the reliability of the ST TO estimations coming from PUSCH and PUCCH transmissions and follows a simple rule. ### 3.11.3.0-2 Computation of Timing Advance Command SRS-based Long-Term TO Computation for TAC (ID: 13442072)

**3.11.3.0-2.0-1**  (ID: `13442074`)

Computation of Timing Advance Command The long term timing power offset parameter represents the reliability of the ST TO estimations coming from PUSCH and SRS transmissions and follows a simple rule. The higher the long term timing power offset the higher the reliability of the corresponding ST TO estimation. Taken into consideration this rule the timing offset (TO) shall be calculated in two consecutive steps. This computation shall only be applicable for UE that has an Scell belonging to a secondary TAG. The TAC shall be sent to the Scell belonging to the sTAG.

With expiry of the timer after rdTacPeriodicity_ms the joint long term timing offset shall be calculated in the ratio of the long term timing power offset related to PUSCH ([11150143](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_ksR__fvSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)) and SRS ([13442072](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_EhoB4CWiEfGxldjOZQLezQ?oslc_config.context=https%3A%2F%2Fdn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com%2Fgc%2Fconfiguration%2F9)) and the number of ST TO estimation from PUSCH (numberOfSamplesFromPusch) and SRS (numberOfSamplesFromSrs). The rule for calculating the ratio is:

  * puschWeight[ue,t]=numberOfSamplesFromPusch*rrmUePuschLongTermOffsetPower[ue,t]
  * srsWeight[ue,t]=numberOfSamplesFromSrs*rrmUeSrsLongTermOffsetPower[ue,t]
  * ratio=puschWeight[ue,t]/(puschWeight[ue,t]+srsWeight[ue,t])



If the parameter rrmUePuschLongTermTimingOffset[ue,t] is INVALID the parameter ratio shall be set to 0 and if rrmUeSrsLongTermTimingOffset[ue,t] is INVALID the parameter ratio shall be set 1. If both, rrmUePuschLongTermTimingOffset[ue,t] and rrmUeSrsLongTermTimingOffset[ue,t] are INVALID the parameter rrmUeLongTermTimingOffset[ue,t] shall be set to 0 to prevent the transmission of a TAC.

The counter numberOfSamplesFromPusch represents the number of ST TO estimations from PUSCH included in rrmUePuschLongTermTimingOffset. So, it shall be incremented whenever rrmUePuschLongTermTimingOffset is updated with ST TO estimations from PUSCH. In addition, it shall be set to zero whenever rrmUePuschLongTermTimingOffset is reset.

Similarly, the counter numberOfSamplesFromSrs represents the number of ST TO estimations from SRS included in rrmUeSrsLongTermTimingOffset. So, it shall be incremented whenever rrmUeSrsLongTermTimingOffset is updated with ST TO estimations from SRS. In addition, it shall be set to zero whenever rrmUeSrsLongTermTimingOffset is reset.

With the ratio related to the long term timing power offset parameters the joint long term timing offset rrmUeLongTermOffset[ue,t] shall be calculated by the following rule:

  * rrmUeLongTermOffset[ue,t]=ratio _rrmUePuschLongTermTimingOffset[ue,t]+(1-ratio)_ rrmUeSrsLongTermTimingOffset[ue,t]



Note: if rrmUePuschLongTermTimingOffset[ue,t] is INVALID or response time-out, rrmUePuschLongTermTimingOffset[ue,t] and puschWeight[ue,t] is set to 0.

## 3.11.4 Accumulated Timing Offset

### 3.11.4.0-1 Accumulated Timing Offset Overview of Accumulated Timing Offset (ID: 11150393)

**3.11.4.0-1.0-2**  (ID: `13470296`)

Accumulated Timing Offset gNB-DU shall maintain the accumulated timing offset (rrmAccumulatedTimingOffset ) for every permanent UE context. The accumulated timing offset is used to deduce the distance between UE and gNB assuming the channel environment is LOS (line-of-sight). 

The accumulated timing offset is sum of:

  * RACH initial TA sent to UE in RACH RAR UL grant

  * All timing adjustments carried in Timing advance command MAC-CE sent to UE

  * Long Term Timing offset measurement




[CB015279-F] For an FR1 UL CA UE which has an activated and time-aligned Scell belonging  
to a secondary timing advance group (TAG), the accumulated timing offset shall only be calculated for the pTAG [End CB015279-F]

### 3.11.4.0-2 Accumulated Timing Offset Initialization of NTA (ID: 11150404)

**3.11.4.0-2.0-1**  (ID: `11150411`)

Accumulated Timing Offset gNB-DU shall maintain NTA in permanent UE context with unit (16*Ts/2. NTA is the accumulated timing offset used by UE in UL PUSCH/PUCCH/SRS transmission according to 38.213 chapter 4.2.

NTA shall be initialized to 0 when the permanent UE context is created, it shall be re-initialized to TACRACH ([11152587: RA Response Timing Advance command](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/TX_OCLOQPvMEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)) with unit 16*Ts/2whenever a PUSCH scheduled by RACH RAR UL grant or DCI 0-0 (in case of re-transmission of msg3) is successfully received from same cell for the same UE.

gNB-DU shall deduce the PUSCH scheduled by RACH RAR UL grant or DCI 0-0 is from the same UE according to:

  * If PUSCH scheduled by RACH RAR UL grant is scrambled by the same RNTI value, or

  * If C-RNTI MAC-CE is carried in PUSCH scheduled by RAR UL grant or DCI 0-0, and contains the same RNTI value. 




gNB-DU may change the temporary UE context to permanent UE context without change of RNTI value, e.g. for SA initial access, in such case, the accumulated timing offset shall be directly initialized to TACRACH ([11152587: RA Response Timing Advance command](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/TX_OCLOQPvMEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)).
