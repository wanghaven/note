# 4.3 PDCP Robust Header Compression (ROHC)

## 4.3.0-2 PDCP Robust Header Compression (ROHC) ROHC Configuration (ID: 11223124)

**4.3.0-2.0-1**  (ID: `11223131`)

PDCP Robust Header Compression (ROHC) 5G-L2-HI shall receive the following parameters at Bearer Setup message:

  * NRBTS.actRohc - flag that indicates if ROHC is enabled or disabled.
  * NRBTS/NRDRB_PDCP.maxCidAllowed - Max number of contexts to be supported per DRB. This is valid only if actRohc is TRUE.



NOTE:

ROHC is currently supported only for 5QI-1 RLC UM mode DRB and by default profiles 0, 1 and 2 are supported.

Hence profiles list is not provided by C plane to 5G-L2-HI.

## 4.3.0-4 PDCP Robust Header Compression (ROHC) ROHC Modes (ID: 11223151)

**4.3.0-4.0-1**  (ID: `11223158`)

PDCP Robust Header Compression (ROHC) 5G-L2-HI shall support the following ROHC modes of operation as described in RFC 3095, RFC 4815:

  * Unidirectional(U-mode)
  * Bidirectional Optimistic(O-mode)
  * Bidirectional Reliable mode(R-mode) - shall be supported only on Compressor side but not on the decompressor side.



NOTE:

Bidirectional Reliable mode(R-mode) is not supported on the Decompressor side since it requires heavy usage of feedback channel which is not efficient.

However, R-mode is supported on Compressor side if UE requests transition to R-mode since compressor cannot reject it.

## 4.3.1 ROHC Compressor

### 4.3.1.0-1 ROHC Compressor Compressor functions (ID: 11223173)

**4.3.1.0-1.0-1**  (ID: `11223179`)

ROHC Compressor ROHC compressor states

---

**4.3.1.0-1.0-1.0-1**  (ID: `11223184`)

ROHC Compressor ROHC compressor operates in 3 states:

  * Initialization and Refresh (IR)
  * First Order (FO)
  * Second Order (SO)



5G-L2-HI shall perform header compression during generation of PDCP PDUs.

5G-L2-HI shall perform the following for Compressor side operation as specified in RFC 3095, RFC 4815:

  * Initially start with U-mode operation and with Initialization and Refresh (IR) state.

  * Transition to different modes is performed based on the feedback provided by the decompressor (UE side)

  * Shall interpret and react according to information provided through feedback packets received by the decompressor (UE side)




\- Shall preform transition (upward/downward) between compressor states, i.e. IR-/ FO-/ SO-state.

Transitions between the various compression states are taken by the compressor on the basis of:

  * Variations in packet headers

  * Positive feedback from decompressor (Acknowledgments -- ACKs)

  * Negative feedback from decompressor (Negative ACKs -- NACKs)

  * Periodic refresh

---

**4.3.1.0-1.0-2**  (ID: `11223191`)

ROHC Compressor Compressor U-mode Operation

---

**4.3.1.0-1.0-2.0-1**  (ID: `11223197`)

ROHC Compressor 5G-L2-HI shall perform upward transition from IR state to FO-state/ SO-state

---

**4.3.1.0-1.0-2.0-1.0-1**  (ID: `11223203`)

ROHC Compressor During initilization, 5G-L2-HI shall send rdRohcFullHdCount (refer 5G_UP_ALG_6021_replaced([11223605](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kyCCrfvSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ))) amount of packets containing full headers.

After 5G-L2-HI has sent rdRohcFullHdCount packets with full header in IR-state and in case dynamic fields update is not required with next IP-packet, 5G-L2-HI shall move directly to SO-state, otherwise to FO-state.

During periodic refresh, 5G-L2-HI shall send one packet containing full header in IR state and move to SO state.

rdRohcFullHdCount is defined in 5G_UP_ALG_6021_replaced([11223605](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kyCCrfvSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)).

---

**4.3.1.0-1.0-2.0-2**  (ID: `11223209`)

ROHC Compressor 5G-L2-HI shall perform upward transition from FO-state to SO-state

---

**4.3.1.0-1.0-2.0-2.0-1**  (ID: `11223217`)

ROHC Compressor 5G-L2-HI shall perform RoHC compression in FO-state, and after sending multiple packets with compressed static header fields in FO state, 5G-L2-HI shall move to SO-state.

---

**4.3.1.0-1.0-2.0-3**  (ID: `11223223`)

ROHC Compressor 5G-L2-HI shall support periodic header refreshes.

---

**4.3.1.0-1.0-2.0-3.0-1**  (ID: `11223228`)

ROHC Compressor 5G-L2-HI shall perform the following:

  * Header refreshes according to RFC 2507.
  * the number of packets received in one period shall be configurable based on the R&D parameter rdRohcHdRefreshPeriod (refer 5G_UP_ALG_6023_replaced([11223617](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kyCCr_vSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)))
  * the header refreshes apply for FO-state and SO-state (i.e. in IR-state full-headers are anyway transmitted)
  * Compressor does not depend upon ACK(U) for state transition since it is optional for decompressor to send the feedback in U mode.

---

**4.3.1.0-1.0-2.0-4**  (ID: `11223236`)

ROHC Compressor 5G-L2-HI shall support downward transition from SO-state to FO-state

---

**4.3.1.0-1.0-2.0-4.0-1**  (ID: `11223241`)

ROHC Compressor The compressor in U-mode operation must periodically move to lower compression states. 

Transition from SO state to FO state shall be performed periodically based on R&D parameter rdRohcHdRefreshPeriod 

rdRohcHdRefreshPeriod is defined in 5G_UP_ALG_6023_replaced([11223617](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kyCCr_vSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)).

---

**4.3.1.0-1.0-2.0-5**  (ID: `11223247`)

ROHC Compressor 5G-L2-HI shall support downward transition from SO-state / FO-state to IR-state

---

**4.3.1.0-1.0-2.0-5.0-1**  (ID: `11223255`)

ROHC Compressor The compressor in U-mode operation must periodically move to IR-state. Transition to IR-state periodicity shall be less compared to the transition to FO-state periodicity.

**4.3.1.0-1.0-3**  (ID: `11223261`)

ROHC Compressor Compressor O-mode Operation

---

**4.3.1.0-1.0-3.0-1**  (ID: `11223267`)

ROHC Compressor The transition into the O-mode is triggered by the decompressor (UE side).

5G-L2-HI shall move to O-mode when ACK(O) is received from decompressor (UE).

---

**4.3.1.0-1.0-4**  (ID: `11223274`)

ROHC Compressor Compressor O-mode state transitions

---

**4.3.1.0-1.0-4.0-1**  (ID: `11223280`)

ROHC Compressor Whenever entered to O-mode 5G-L2-HI shall first compress IP packets in a state used in a previous mode.

In O-mode feedback about successful / unsuccessful decompression shall be received from the decompressor. Sending of positive feedback about successful compression is determined optional in RFC3095 and thus it will not be always received.

In case 5G-L2-HI performs RoHC compression in IR/FO-state, and if ACK(O)- feedback has been received or rdRohcUpwSO (refer 5G_UP_ALG_6038_replaced([11223704](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kyCpvPvSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ))) amount of packets have been sent in the current state, 5G-L2-HI shall move to SO-state.

In case 5G-L2-HI performs RoHC compression in SO-state, and if NACK(O)- feedback has been received,5G-L2-HI shall move to FO-state and send the updates to decompressor as defined in RFC3095.

In case 5G-L2-HI performs RoHC compression in FO- or SO-state, and if STATIC NACK(O)- feedback has been received, 5G-L2-HI shall move to IR-state.

---

**4.3.1.0-1.0-5**  (ID: `11223287`)

ROHC Compressor Compressor R-mode Operation

---

**4.3.1.0-1.0-5.0-1**  (ID: `11223294`)

ROHC Compressor The transition to the R-mode is triggered by the decompressor (UE side).

5G-L2-HI shall move to R-mode when ACK(R) is received from decompressor (UE).

gNB shall support R-mode on compressor side since mode transition request from decompressor (i.e. UE side) cannot be rejected by compressor (i.e. gNB side) as per ROHC algorithm described in RFC3095.

---

**4.3.1.0-1.0-6**  (ID: `11223301`)

ROHC Compressor Compressor R-mode state transitions

---

**4.3.1.0-1.0-6.0-1**  (ID: `11223307`)

ROHC Compressor Whenever entered to R-mode 5G-L2-HI shall first compress IP packets in a state which was used in a previous mode.

In R-mode feedback about successful / unsuccessful decompression shall be received from the decompressor. Note that ACK(R)-feedback is valid only in case it matches to a sent SN and otherwise, 5G-L2-HI shall reject the received ACK(R).

In case 5G-L2-HI performs RoHC compression in IR-state, and if ACK(R)- feedback has been received, 5G-L2-HI shall move to FO-state.

In case 5G-L2-HI performs RoHC compression in FO-state, and if ACK(R)- feedback has been received, 5G-L2-HI shall move to SO-state.

In case 5G-L2-HI performs RoHC compression in SO-state, and if NACK(R)- feedback has been received, 5G-L2-HI shall move to FO-state and updates to decompressor as defined in RFC3095.

In case 5G-L2-HI performs RoHC compression in FO- or SO-state, and if STATIC NACK(R)- feedback has been received, 5G-L2-HI shall move to IR-state.

---

**4.3.1.0-1.0-7**  (ID: `11223313`)

ROHC Compressor SDU discard timer implication on Compressor state transitions

---

**4.3.1.0-1.0-7.0-1**  (ID: `11223321`)

ROHC Compressor SDU discard timer is handled at 5G-L2-LO (RLC) since PDCP does not buffer the PDCP PDUs for RLC UM mode DRB since flow control is disabled.

RLC PDUs may be dropped at 5G-L2-LO if the SDU discard timer expires. If the consecutive compressed packets are dropped at 5G-L2-LO (RLC) due to SDU discard timer expiry, then the compressor(gNB) and decompressor (UE) will be out of sync. Hence the R&D parameter values for rdRohcFullHdCount and rdRohcUpwSO should be set considering the SDU discard timer function at 5G-L2-LO.

rdRohcUpwSO is defined in 5G_UP_ALG_6038_replaced([11223704](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kyCpvPvSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)).

rdRohcFullHdCount is defined in 5G_UP_ALG_6021_replaced([11223605](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kyCCrfvSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)).

## 4.3.3 ROHC Context Handling

### 4.3.3.0-1 ROHC Context Handling ROHC contexts per Data Radio Bearer (DRB) (ID: 11223466)

**4.3.3.0-1.0-1**  (ID: `11223473`)

ROHC Context Handling 5G-L2-HI shall maintain RoHC session contexts assigned to the DRBs.

When the compression profile 1 (RTP/UDP/IP) is activated the RTP sessions are kept in separate RoHC contexts. There is unique RoHC context for each unique RTP:SSRC value.

When a RTP packet is received, 5G-L2-HI shall verify if this packet can be processed by any context within RTP contexts subset of given DRB.

In case 5G-L2-HI detects that incoming RTP packet has header fields different than all the maintained ones for this DRB (except sequence number), new unused context for this session is allocated and profile 1 shall be used.

When a RTCP packet is received, 5G-L2-HI shall use profile 2 and allocate a new unused context if the packet cannot be processed using existing RTCP contexts.

When a received packet is neither RTP, RTCP or UDP, profile 0 shall be used.

When a received packet has unidentified header (like Ethernet header, corrupted header, etc.,),

ROHC compressor shall discard the DL PDCP SDU.

If there is no free context available, and the RoHC context reuse mechanism described in 5G_UP_ALG_6004_replaced([11223499](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kyBbnvvSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)) shall be performed.

Note:

  * According to RFC3095 when profile 1 is available and SSRC field of RTP stream changes, RoHC compressor tries to allocate separate RoHC context for the stream.

---

### 4.3.3.0-2 ROHC Context Handling ROHC maximum number of contexts (ID: 11223480)

**4.3.3.0-2.0-2**  (ID: `11223492`)

ROHC Context Handling 5G-L2-HI has buffer limitation for supporting the number of ROHC contexts across all DRBs supported by the instance.

If the maximum number of ROHC contexts is already allocated for the DRBs and if a new bearer setup request is received,

5G-L2-HI shall accept the bearer setup but will send data using uncompressed profile.

5G-L2-HI shall provide the maximum number of ROHC contexts that can be supported per Bearer group to C plane via [Before CNI-88099-F] HiCnfgCu_AddressDistributionResp [CNI-88099-F] 5G-L2-HI CU Address Distribution Response [End CNI-88099-F].

### 4.3.4.0-4 ROHC Context Re-use ROHC Context re-use steps (ID: 11223542)

**4.3.4.0-4.0-1**  (ID: `11223549`)

ROHC Context Re-use 5G-L2-HI shall perform RoHC context re-use procedure as described below:

Within a DRB, apply IP header checking of incoming RTP- and RTCP- packets. (e.g. IPv4: addresses, protocol, e.g. IPv6: addresses, flowlabel, next header)  


If the new incoming RTP packet consists of header field values (except sequence number) are different than the existing and allocated contexts a new unused context for this session is allocated.  


If context resources are exhausted, the RoHC context re-use mechanism shall be applied as follows:  
\- in case of new RTP packet:

  * in case of RTCP packet:

Note: The oldest RTP-context in operation is the one which has not received any packets for the longest time compared to the other active RTP sessions in this DRB.  
The oldest RTCP-context in operation is the one which has not received any packets for the longest time compared to the other active RTCP sessions in the DRB.

## 4.3.5 HFN Recovery procedure

### 4.3.5.0-1 HFN Recovery procedure Overview (ID: 11223574)

**4.3.5.0-1.0-1**  (ID: `11223580`)

HFN Recovery procedure HFN (Hyper Frame Number) recovery mechanism is required for the uplink direction for RLC UM mode DRBs to detect and recover the potential HFN desynchronization when ciphering/ de-ciphering is enabled for the DRB.

In this HFN recovery procedure, positive result of RoHC decompression of PDCP SDU will be used for verification if ROHC is enabled for the DRB. For the RLC UM mode DRB with ROHC disabled, verification relies on assumption that all the packets sent over DRB must be IPv4 or IPv6 packets.

The HFN recovery algorithm tries to guess the correct HFN typically using one after the other: current HFN-1, current HFN+1, current HFN+2 and decipher PDCP PDU with a candidate HFN. If the packet validation succeeds with candidate HFN, 5G-L2-HI algorithm assumes that the candidate HFN might be the correct one.

In case when the problem with traffic is not solved by the HFN recovery mechanism, and the situation does not normalize by itself, an intra-cell handover procedure is triggered to reset PDCP HFN, SN and RoHC contexts.

Exceptions:

  * Intra-cell handover procedure when the HFN recovery fails is not applicable for RLC AM bearer because PDCP SN/ HFN is not reset during handover procedure.

  * If the RoHC decompression fails due to RoHC specific conditions, the recovery on RoHC level may take more than 10 packets. In such cases the HFN recovery will not help as the problem is not related to deciphering.
