# 4.7 PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm

## 4.7.0-2 PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm Discard DL PDCP SDU due to buffer overflow (ID: 11223925)

**4.7.0-2.0-1**  (ID: `11223930`)

PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm If pdcpBufDiscardEnabled is set to True and the sum of "buffered PDCP SDU(s)" and "PDCP PDU(s) waiting for ack from RLC entity" for the specific bearer has reached or exceeded the value of pdcpBufDiscardThreshold, 5G-L2-HI shall discard new incoming DL PDCP SDU(s). The discarding is continued as long is pdcpBufDiscardThreshold is exceeded.

pdcpBufDiscardEnabled and pdcpBufDiscardThreshold is vendor specific parameter received at Bearer Setup.

5G-L2-HI shall start to drop new incoming DL PDCP SDU(s) and UL PDCP PDU(s) when PDCP buffer is full(memory limitation).

The PDCP buffer overflow can happen due to High Rate of incoming DL PDCP SDU and UL PDCP PDU.

The PDCP buffer is shared among different DRBs and shall be maintained at 5G-L2-HI level.

## 4.7.0-5 PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm PI2 based Delay Based Discarding (ID: 11223963)

**4.7.0-5.0-1**  (ID: `11223971`)

PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm 5G-L2-HI shall support PI2 based Delay Based Discarding

---

**4.7.0-5.0-1.0-2**  (ID: `11223982`)

PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm Before the PDCP SDU is assigned a sequence number, 5G-L2-HI decides whether to discard the packet based on the PI2 drop probability pi2_p. The pi2_p is compared to a uniform random variable u and the packet is discarded if:

pi2_p > u and qDelay >[CB010936-A-UPD003]=[End CB010936-A-UPD003] rdPi2DropThreshold * pi2TargetDelay

Note that packets with a PDCP SN already assigned (due to retransmission or forwarding, etc) shall not be dropped and will not be checked for discard.

In addition, packet discarding for new or resumed radio bearers shall be deferred for NRDRB.queuingDelayDiscardStart.

The rdPi2DropThreshold is an R&D parameter that will range from 0 to 100% and is initially set to 75%.

[CB010936-A]

If NRBTS/NRDRB_ L4S.l4sDlVariant > 0, the rdPi2DropThreshold takes the value of another R&D parameter - rdL4SDropThreshold initially set to 25%.

[End CB010936-A]

---

**4.7.0-5.0-2**  (ID: `11223990`)

PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm PI2 queue management - drop probability p calculation

---

**4.7.0-5.0-2.0-2**  (ID: `11224003`)

PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm The drop probability pi2_p is calculated on every DDDS arrival, regardless of whether it comes from LTE or NR, and at least every 5 ms, as follows:

tUpdate = now - prev_time;

prev_time = now;

delayScaledMaxSize = qSize * pi2TargetDelay / pi2TargetSize

[Before CB010936-A]

delayScaledMinSize = qSize * pi2TargetDelay / rdPi2TargetMinSize

[End CB010936-A]

if (qDelay < delayScaledMaxSize)

piDelay = delayScaledMaxSize

[Before CB010936-A]

else if (qDelay > delayScaledMinSize)

piDelay = delayScaledMinSize

[End CB010936-A]

else

piDelay = qDelay

Δ = α ∗ tUpdate * (piDelay − pi2TargetDelay) + β ∗ (piDelay − prev_piDelay)

prev_piDelay = piDelay

p' = prev_p' + Δ

p' = min(max(rdPi2MinPprime, p'), 1)

prev_p' = p'

And then:

clamped_p' = max(0, p')

pi2_p = clamped_p' * clamped_p' 

5G-L2-HI derives the qDelay as described in 5G_UP_ALG_28563_replaced([11224011](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kyFF8PvSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)).

The qSize shall be derived as the CU buffer occupancy (only PDCP SDUs) of that bearer.

[Before CB010936-A]

The rdPi2TargetMinSize is an R&D parameter that will range from 1 to 100 and is initially set to 1 Kbit.

[End CB010936-A]

The rdPi2MinPprime is an R&D parameter that will range from -1 to 0 and is initially set to -0.05.

[CB010936-A]

If NRBTS/NRDRB_ L4S.l4sDlVariant > 0, the rdPi2MinPprime takes the value of another R&D parameter - rdL4sMinPprime [Before PR813944]initially set to 0[PR813944]set by default to -0.05[End PR813944].

[End CB010936-A]

The NRDRB.pi2TargetDelay is the NIDD parameter in ms, with the values between 1 and 5000. The pi2TargetDelay default is 900ms.

The NRDRB.pi2TargetSize is the NIDD parameter in Mbit (Megabit), with the values between 1 and 5000. The pi2TargetSize default is 50Mbit.

The other parameters will be derived as follows:

rtt_max = max(2 * pi2TargetDelay, pi2TargetDelay+ 0.1s) (Worst case RTT expected in ms)

α = 1 / rtt_max^2 / 10 (PI2 integral gain in (kHz)²)

β = 3 / rtt_max / 10 (PI2 proportional gain in kHz)

**4.7.0-5.0-3**  (ID: `11224011`)

PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm Queue delay calculation

---

**4.7.0-5.0-3.0-1**  (ID: `11224018`)

PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm 5G-L2-HI derives the queue delay as the sum of the age of the oldest packet in the CU buffer plus estimated RLC delay (DU/LTE), which is determined as the minimum of the delays on individual paths that have instEstTput > 0\. If there is no path with instEstTput > 0, then the RLC delay (DU/LTE) will be kept at the level calculated previously.

Notes:

  * The estimated RLC delay will be slightly larger than the real RLC queuing delay as it will also include the F1/X2 RTT.

## 4.7.0-6 PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm 5G-L2-HI shall support DL PDCP SDU discarding based on the queuing delay for DRBs (ID: 11224024)

**4.7.0-6.0-2**  (ID: `11224037`)

PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm For the DRBs with non-empty PDCP SDU buffer,

5G-L2-HI shall time stamp the DL PDCP SDUs received over the S1-U/Ng-U or X2-U (data forwarding) to measure the queuing delay. The queuing delay is calculated as the time difference between the current time instance and the time instant of reception of the DL PDCP SDU [CB008225-B-UPD003] if the bearer is marked as "active" or the time instant of the transition of the bearer from "inactive to "active". Refer 5G_UP_ALG_16585_replaced([11228450](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kyiY9_vSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)) for bearer inactive state transitions [End CB008225-B-UPD003].

On expiry of the timer tDiscardQueueCheck, 5G-L2-HI shall check the queuing delay of the first (i.e. oldest) unsent PDCP SDU in the data bearer data buffer.

IF queuing delay > queuingDelaySduDiscardThreshold

  * Stop the periodic timer tDiscardQueueCheck.
  * Check and discard up to numSduDiscard PDCP SDU(s), whose queuing delay is exceeding the queuingDelaySduDiscardThreshold.
  * Start the timer tdiscardQueueMonitor. The initial value of this timer shall be set as per the parameter sduDiscardIntervalTmr.
  * Set N=1.



ELSE

  * End the procedure.



END

On expiry of the tdiscardQueueMonitor , 5G-L2-HI shall check the queuing delay of the first (i.e. oldest) unsent PDCP SDU in the data bearer data buffer.

IF queuing delay > queuingDelaySduDiscardThreshold

 Check and discard up to numSduDiscard PDCP SDU(s) whose queuing delay is exceeding the queuingDelaySduDiscardThreshold.

 IF N < 10

Increment N by 1.

 Start the timer tdiscardQueueMonitor with the value of sduDiscardIntervalTmr/Sqrt(N)

ELSE

 Set N = 0;

 Re-start the periodic timer tDiscardQueueCheck (5G_UP_ALG_5937_replaced([11223945](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kyEe5vvSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ))).

END

queuingDelaySduDiscardThreshold, sduDiscardIntervalTmr and numSduDiscard are parameters received at Bearer Setup.

## 4.7.0-7 PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm discardTimer based SDU discard for DRB with flow control disabled (ID: 11224043)

**4.7.0-7.0-1**  (ID: `11224050`)

PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm PDCP discardTimer based SDU discard for DRB with flow control disabled shall be done at L2-LO RLC.

Rationale: For DRB with Flow control disabled, PDCP transmits PDU immediately after receiving it over NG-U interface and does not store PDCP SDU or PDU in PDCP buffer.

The following are the packet discard requirements for DRB with flow control disabled

  1. Every DL RLC PDU shall have a timestamp that specifies the time when the packet was received at RLC. This timestamp will be used to determine if a packet is older than the PDCP discardTimer.

  2. When L2-LO receives LoCtrl_PduMuxReq from L2-PS, L2-LO will check if the candidate RLC PDUs have exceeded the PDCP discardTimer. If an RLC PDU is older than the discard timer value the following shall be done:




\- The RLC PDU shall be discarded and not inserted into the MAC PDU which is to be delivered to physical layer

\- L2-LO shall record in the shared memory how many RLC PDUs have been discarded and how many RLC PDUs have been delivered to physical layer

Note - If an RLC PDU has been segmented and at least one of the segments has been transmitted prior to the PDCP discardTimer expiry then all segments will be transmitted.

## 4.7.0-8 PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm Selection Criteria for Priority based discarding (ID: 11224057)

**4.7.0-8.0-2**  (ID: `13399841`)

PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm When buffer congestion or buffer overflow based discarding is triggered, gNB-CU-UP (for PDCP) or gNB-DU (for RLC) shall:

  * [CB014699-D] gNB-CU-UP (PDCP) or gNB-DU (RLC) shall not select bearers marked for critical traffic (NRBTS.NRDRB.criticalDrbSchedEnabled set to True) for discard if there is a mixture of non-critical and critical traffic. However, if all the bearers are marked Critical or only Critical traffic bearers have data (non-Critical bearers do not have data at the time of congestion or buffer overflow), then the below conditions apply for the selection of bearers for discarding. [End CB014699-D]
  * Amongst the bearers (non-GBR and GBR) with Priority Level (refer Note) greater than or equal to 60, select the bearer with the highest buffer occupancy. non-GBR bearers shall be considered before GBR bearers.
  * If no bearers exist with priority level greater than or equal to 60, amongst the bearers (non-GBR and GBR) with Priority Level greater than 30, select the bearer with the highest buffer occupancy.



Note:

  * Priority Level (NIDD parameter NRBTS.NRDRB.priorityLevel) for bearers are shared as part of bearer setup procedure by Cplane.
  * The optimal procedure for realisation of the above Priority based selection, considering the memory and performance impacts, is left to implementation.
  * Bearers with Priority level less than or equal to 30 shall be excluded from bearer selection procedure. DC-GBR (5QI 82-88) and MC-GBR (5QI 65,66,67) are excluding from selection for discarding.
  * gNB-CU-UP and gNB-DU shall use a default value of PriorityLevel for non-GBR (set to 127) and GBR (set to 56) for bearers for whom the NIDD parameter **NRBTS.NRDRB.priorityLevel may not have been set. This helps in the case of online enable of Priority based buffer congestion and overload handling.**

## 4.7.0-9 PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm PDCP SDU discard due to PDCP Buffer congestion mechanism (ID: 11224070)

**4.7.0-9.0-4**  (ID: `13399853`)

PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm 5G-L2-HI shall detect PDCP Buffer(SDU and PDU) congestion on reception of new PDCP SDU when one of the below condition is met

  * The received PDCP SDU is for DC-GBR DRB **AND PDCP Buffer (SDU and PDU) usage exceeds the PDCP buffer usage threshold.**



**Note: NRBTS.actPdcpRlcBufCongOvlMech is by default enabled if DC-GBR feature is activated.**

**OR**

The received PDCP SDU is for GBR DRB marked as “active” **AND PDCP Buffer(SDU and PDU) usage exceeds the PDCP buffer usage threshold** AND "GBR" bit is set in the NRBTS.actPdcpRlcBufCongOvlMech parameter****

**OR**

  * The received PDCP SDU is for non-GBR DRB marked as “active” **AND PDCP Buffer(SDU and PDU) usage exceeds the PDCP buffer usage threshold** AND**"NonGBR" bit is set in the NRBTS.actPdcpRlcBufCongOvlMech parameter** ****



**OR**

  * The received PDCP SDU is for DRB marked as “inactive” **AND PDCP Buffer(SDU and PDU) usage exceeds the PDCP buffer usage threshold** AND "RRCInactive" bit is set in the NRBTS.actPdcpRlcBufCongOvlMech****



**NOTE:**

MC-GBR DRB (5QI 65, 66, 67) are handled similar to other GBR DRB with no specific extension for the above trigger conditions for discards.

[CB014699-D] gNB-CU-UP shall exclude bearers which are marked for critical traffic (i.e, NRBTS.NRDRB.criticalDrbSchedEnabled set to True), from consideration for discard unless all active bearers are marked for critical traffic or only Critical traffic bearers have data (non-Critical bearers do not have data at the time of congestion or buffer overflow). [End CB014699-D]

The following action shall be taken when PDCP Buffer(SDU and PDU) congestion is detected

**IF incoming PDCP SDU is for DC-GBR DRB, select the non-GBR bearer having the highest number of unsent PDCP SDU(s). If only GBR bearers are present, select the GBR bearer having highest number of unsent PDCP SDU(s). MC-GBR bearers are excluded from discarding. If the "Priority" bit is set in NRBTS.actPdcpRlcBufCongOvlMech parameter, then the bearer shall be selected according to 5G_UP_ALG_31442_replaced([11224057](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kyFF9_vSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)).**

**ELSE:**

  * Select the non-GBR Bearer having highest number of unsent PDCP SDU(s). If the "Priority" bit is set in NRBTS.actPdcpRlcBufCongOvlMech parameter, then the bearer shall be selected according to 5G_UP_ALG_31442_replaced([11224057](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kyFF9_vSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)).

  * Discard "NRBTS.numNonGBRPdcpSduDiscard" PDCP SDU(s) of the selected DRB.




PDCP buffer usage threshold = ("NRBTS.pdcpBufUsageThreshold" * max PDCP buffer size)/100

Note: PDCP SDU which have SN already assigned shall not be discarded.

NRBTS.pdcpBufUsageThreshold is a NIDD parameter.

NRBTS.numNonGBRPdcpSduDiscard is a NIDD parameter.

**Note: NRBTS.actPdcpRlcBufCongOvlMech is by default enabled if DC-GBR feature is activated.**

Rationale

  1. Discarding of PDCP SDU for non-GBR DRB is done to allow buffer availabiltiy for GBR DRB. This enables GBR prioritization wrt PDCP Buffer.

  2. Discarding of PDCP SDU for non-GBR DRB with highest PDCP SDU is done to allow to ensure fair utilization of PDCP Buffer among non-GBR DRB's.

  3. DC-GBR bearers are prioritized over the non-GBR and other GBR services.

## 4.7.0-10 PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm 5G-L2-HI (CU) Buffer Overflow Protection (ID: 11224100)

**4.7.0-10.0-1**  (ID: `11224105`)

PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm 5G-L2-HI (CU) overflow protection at critical level

---

**4.7.0-10.0-1.0-2**  (ID: `11224121`)

PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm When the L2 (SDAP for SA or PDCP for NSA) receives an SDU from the Core and the BIP Pool has only bipPoolCriticalMargin free descriptors, 5G-L2-HI shall discard this SDU and peg the DL_SDU_DISC_CRIT_BOP_CU counter.

[CB008600-B]

gNB-CU-UP (5G-L2-Hi) shall not discard the SDU if it is for DC-GBR bearer.

[End CB008600-B]

[CB008118-A]

gNB-CU-UP (5G-L2-Hi) shall not discard the SDU if it is for MC-GBR bearer.

[End CB008118-A]

The 5G-L2-HI (CU) will calculate bipPoolCriticalMargin value depending on the used configuration.

---

**4.7.0-10.0-2**  (ID: `11224129`)

PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm 5G-L2-HI (CU) overflow protection at non-critical level

---

**4.7.0-10.0-2.0-3**  (ID: `13399859`)

PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm When the PDCP receives an SDU and in  _NRBTS.actPdcpRlcBufCongOvlMech, "BufferOverflow" bit is set_

gNB-CU-UP shall perform the actions mentioned below if EITHER of the following conditions is satisfied:

  * [CB014699-D] gNB-CU-UP shall exclude bearers that are marked for critical traffic (i.e, NRBTS.NRDRB.criticalDrbSchedEnabled set to True) from consideration for discard unless all active bearers are marked for critical traffic or only Critical traffic bearers have data (non-Critical bearers do not have data at the time of congestion or buffer overflow). [End CB014699-D]
  * IF PDCP Buffer usage for a given Bearer Group is greater than or equal to NRBTS.pdcpBufOverflowThreshold.
  * IF the number of descriptors occupied in the BIP Pool is equal to or above the _bipPoolNonCriticalMargin. (refer Notes)_



Actions:

 Identify the DRB (in the bearer group) which is **not DC-GBR and which is** not MC-GBR DRB and**occupies the most of BIP pool resources. If the "Priority" bit is set in NRBTS.actPdcpRlcBuffeCongestionMechsm parameter, then the bearer shall be selected according to 5G_UP_ALG_31442_replaced([11224057](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kyFF9_vSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)).******

 Set the dropDepth variable initially to _NRBTS.bopNumPdcpSduDiscard, otherwise recalculate the dropDepth as 2 * ('previous dropDepth' - 'ms since the last discarding by this procedure')._

 Clamp the dropDepth between _NRBTS.bopNumPdcpSduDiscard and 50% of the pool occupancy by this bearer._

 Discard the dropDepth SDUs from the bearer occupying the largest part of the BIP pool.

 Peg the DL_SDU_DISC_BOP_CU counter.

Notes:

 The procedure operates at the bearer group level, so that the dropDepth variable is maintained per group.

 It may happen that the heaviest bearer will belong to a different bearer group, but the heaviest bearer will receive the most packets, so it will be the most impacted anyway.

 When it comes to the pool occupancy, 5G-L2-HI (CU) takes into account only PDCP SDUs.

  * If performance is not sufficient, 5G-L2-HI (CU) can run this procedure every few packets.
  * _bipPoolNonCriticalMargin is an internal parameter whose value shall be calculated based on the BIP pool configuration such that it shall be lesser than or equal to the_ bipPoolCriticalMargin**
  * VoNR bearers are not explicitly excluded from the discarding as their buffering is low or non-existent due to flow control not enabled, they would not be the contributing bearers for the overflow and hence not impacted.

## 4.7.0-11 PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm PDCP SDU discard due to PDCP timer based discard (ID: 11224150)

**4.7.0-11.0-2**  (ID: `11224161`)

"sojourn time" = current time - time when PDCP SDU was received at 5G-L2-HI [CB008225-B-UPD003] if the bearer is marked as "active" or the time instant of the transition of the bearer from "inactive to "active". ## 4.7.0-12 PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm PDCP SDU discard due to maximum PDCP SDU/T-PDU size or PDCP Control PDU size (ID: 11224167)

**4.7.0-12.0-1**  (ID: `11224174`)

PDCP SDU Discard Algorithm 5G-L2-HI shall not support super jumbo frames.

In the UL, The maximum PDCP SDU/T-PDU size shall be restricted to 9000 bytes.
