**Below statements explain why "normalization factor" is needed and how it is calculated. L1 DL shall need to follow the rule to generate precoding matrix in precoding stage.**

The precoding matrices A, used both in DL and UL and obtained through various mathematical techniques, often need to be normalized by a so-called “normalization factor” ρ so that the normalized precoding matrices W exhibit some power conservation properties (that will be detailed in the sequel). The following notations will be used:[image-0][image\_desc]The image displays a mathematical equation involving matrices.
The equation is:
$W = \rho \cdot A$
Where:
$W$ is an $n \times m$ matrix with elements $W\_{i,j}$ for $i$ from 0 to $n-1$ and $j$ from 0 to $m-1$.
$\rho$ is a scalar.
$A$ is an $n \times m$ matrix with elements $a\_{i,j}$ for $i$ from 0 to $n-1$ and $j$ from 0 to $m-1$.
The matrix $W$ is represented as:
$$
W = \begin{pmatrix}
W\_{0,0} & W\_{0,1} & \cdots & W\_{0,m-1} \
W\_{1,0} & W\_{1,1} & \cdots & W\_{1,m-1} \
\vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \
W\_{n-1,0} & W\_{n-1,1} & \cdots & W\_{n-1,m-1}
\end{pmatrix}
$$
The matrix $A$ is represented as:
$$
A = \begin{pmatrix}
a\_{0,0} & a\_{0,1} & \cdots & a\_{0,m-1} \
a\_{1,0} & a\_{1,1} & \cdots & a\_{1,m-1} \
\vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \
a\_{n-1,0} & a\_{n-1,1} & \cdots & a\_{n-1,m-1}
\end{pmatrix}
$$

**Below statements explain why "normalization factor" is needed and how it is calculated. L1 DL shall need to follow the rule to generate precoding matrix in precoding stage.**

The precoding matrices A, used both in DL and UL and obtained through various mathematical techniques, often need to be normalized by a so-called “normalization factor” ρ so that the normalized precoding matrices W exhibit some power conservation properties (that will be detailed in the sequel). The following notations will be used:[image-0][image\_desc]The image displays a mathematical equation involving matrices.
The equation is:
$W = \rho \cdot A$
Where:
$W$ is an $n \times m$ matrix with elements $W\_{i,j}$ for $i$ from 0 to $n-1$ and $j$ from 0 to $m-1$.
$\rho$ is a scalar.
$A$ is an $n \times m$ matrix with elements $a\_{i,j}$ for $i$ from 0 to $n-1$ and $j$ from 0 to $m-1$.
The matrix $W$ is represented as:
$$
W = \begin{pmatrix}
W\_{0,0} & W\_{0,1} & \cdots & W\_{0,m-1} \
W\_{1,0} & W\_{1,1} & \cdots & W\_{1,m-1} \
\vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \
W\_{n-1,0} & W\_{n-1,1} & \cdots & W\_{n-1,m-1}
\end{pmatrix}
$$
The matrix $A$ is represented as:
$$
A = \begin{pmatrix}
a\_{0,0} & a\_{0,1} & \cdots & a\_{0,m-1} \
a\_{1,0} & a\_{1,1} & \cdots & a\_{1,m-1} \
\vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \
a\_{n-1,0} & a\_{n-1,1} & \cdots & a\_{n-1,m-1}
\end{pmatrix}
$$
The equation signifies that matrix $W$ is obtained by multiplying each element of matrix $A$ by the scalar $\rho$.[/image\_desc][image\_path]https://storage.googleapis.com/dng\_files/images/HTML\_pukkola\_5G\_L1\_Entity\_Level\_file\_2866.001.png[/image\_path][/image-0]     (equation 2.3.2-1)

where:

m is the rank or number of layers;

n is the number of “physical” antenna ports;

W is the normalized (n,m) precoding matrix;

$\langle |y\_i|^2 \rangle = \langle |\sum\_{j=0}^{m-1} a\_{i,j} \cdot x\_j|^2 \rangle$[/image\_desc][image\_path]https://storage.googleapis.com/dng\_files/images/HTML\_pukkola\_5G\_L1\_Entity\_Level\_file\_2866.005.png[/image\_path][/image-4]      (equation 2.3.2-2)

## 2.3.5 RU gain and reference average "digital power" at the BBU output

### 2.3.5.1 Common for both CPRI and eCPRI

**2.3.5.1.0-1**  (ID: `11417230`)

Common for both CPRI and eCPRI
The fixed RU gain is defined by associating a reference average “digital” OFDM symbol power Pavg at the output of the BBU to a configured total RF Tx power per polarization Ptot at the output of the RU. In other words, whenever the digital power at the BBU output is Pavg, then the RF power per polarization at the output of the RU is Ptot.

If the EPRE for a physical channel or signal m has to be αm.Ptot at the RF output, it then means it has to be set to αm.Pavg in L1 (it could be in the RE mapping stage or combined with precoding stage and depends on actual implementation).

L2 does not need to know about Pavg, but L1 uses Pavg in order to perform the appropriate power scaling of physical signals and channels.

The way Pavg is defined is different depending on whether a CPRI interface or an eCPRI interface is used between the BBU and the RU. This is explained in the next two sections.

1) Q 1.1.13: S = 2^1 \* 10^(-18.2/20) ; Power in dBFs = 20\*LOG10(2^1 \* 10^(-18.2/20) \* 2^13 / 2^14) = -18.2 dBFs

2) Q 1.1.14: S = 2^1 \* 10^(-18.2/20) ; Power in dBFs = 20\*LOG10(2^1 \* 10^(-18.2/20) \* 2^14 / 2^15) = -18.2 dBFs

Option 1 happens to be OK for CPRI-N with -18.2 dBFs@15 bit and option 2 happens to be OK for RP3 with -18.2 dBFs@16 bit. Option 2 is used in Loki, so for other FH interfaces, data conversion is needed.

To be compatible with CPRI-N, CPRI-A and OBSAI fronthaul interfaces, one common power scaling by equation 2.3.6.1.3-1 is applied before FH interface with a = 1 and B = 18.2. That’s to say, the rms IQ level shall equal to 4024[dec] before data sending to CPRI or RP3 with 16 bit width, then:

1) If the FH interface is RP3, then no additional data format conversion is needed, and the power level shall be -18.2 dBFs (4024@16 bit);

2) if the FH interface is CPRI interface, then the alignment functionality provided by CTX\_ULAW\_GENERAL registers can be used to select which bit of 16 bits to be dropped to get 15 bits CPRI data. **Please note that the register is link specific.**

2a) If it is CPRI-N, then the alignment field is set to zero, the least significant bit of 16 bits RP3 sample is truncated, and the power level shall be -18.2 dBFs (2012@15 bit);

2b) if it is CPRI-A, then the alignment field is set to one, the most significant value bit of RP3 sample is discarded and the power level shall be -12.2 dBFs (4024@15  bit).

**[End PR649434]**

**1. In Odin, DL\_SYMGEN produces QAM symbols as fixed point format Q1.0.15, while 64 and 256QAM may have real or imaginary part greater than 1, then an additional downscaling is done to store the values, and The scaling values are:**

64QAM: 1 / {1 / Sqrt(42) \* (4 + 3) \* (2^15 -1) / 2^15}

256QAM: 1 / {1 / Sqrt(170) \* (8 + 4 + 3) \* (2^15 -1) / 2^15}

so this downscaling needs to be reversed with the re-scaled data channel scaling value.

**2. In Odin, modulation symbol is a fixed value for I and Q of (+/-) 32767 for reference signal channels (which are used for DMRS and PTRS), so additional scaling 1/sqrt(2) needs to be compensated.**
