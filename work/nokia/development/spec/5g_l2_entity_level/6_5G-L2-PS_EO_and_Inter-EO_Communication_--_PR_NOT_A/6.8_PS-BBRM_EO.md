### 6.8.2.3 Metric Management

#### 6.8.2.3.0-1 Metric Management Steps of the load based algorithm (ID: 11246445)

**6.8.2.3.0-1.0-1**  (ID: `11246452`)

Metric Management [5GC001227-B]

The global load based algorithm can be described by the following steps :

1) Metric preparation (5G_L2_4580_replaced([11174638](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_26luAvvTEe-AqvopbP1qhQ))) on a pooling period (P)

2) Metric Ind sending (5G_L2_4580_replaced([11174638](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_26luAvvTEe-AqvopbP1qhQ))) on slot 0 of a pooling period (P+1)

\-----> steps perform by the UL & DL Schedulers

3) Calculation of Resource allocation, using metrics, on 1st Resource Req received in the pooling period (P+1) for the L1 SP and feed back to scheduler in Resource Resp

\-----> step perform by BBRM 

4) Application of new allocated resources at next SFN

\-----> steps perform by the UL & DL Schedulers

Note : The PRB values sent by BBRM are applied at the next SFN and for the duration of the pooling period: so shifted from one SFN compare to the SFNs where the metrics are elaborated.

For example with a pooling period of 5 SFNs, the metrics are elaborated from SFN x to SFN x+4 and sent to BBRM at slot 0 of SFN x+5. BBRM corresponding limits are applied by the schedulers at SFN x+6 to SFN x+10 while the new metrics are elaborated from SFN x+5 to SFN x+9.

## 6.8.4 Scheduling of L1 Processing Resources

### 6.8.4.1 Common

#### 6.8.4.1.0-1 Common Handling of missed Transmission of ResourceReq Message by the Scheduler (ID: 11246573)

**6.8.4.1.0-1.0-3**  (ID: `11246592`)

Common Due to the overload control kicking in, it is possible that an entire slot is skip for the scheduling (DL or UL).

The slot number for the ResourceReq transmission is hardcoded in L2-PS SW (resReqTxSlotNo). If that slot is skip, then also resourceReq message would not be sent. In turn the scheduler (DL or UL) shall not receive the corresponding resourceResp, which could carry an updated resource share, compared to the previous pooling period. In the worst case, this can cause the L1 pooling capacity to be exceeded.

As mitigation action, each DL/UL scheduler, shall check if the sending of the resourceReq in resReqTxSlotNo has been skip and, in that case, trigger the sending of the message in the next possible slots of that SFN.

In the SFN where BBRM has to execute the intra pooling algorithms (PRB, UE, ...), all the schedulers (DL and UL) of all the cells should execute a resourceReq/resouceResp exchange of messages. (In case of DL PRB pooling, this exchange should be executed prior to the max k2 value before of the SFN transition).

[Before CNI-127661]

If, due to an overload situation and skip of slots, the above processing cannot be fullfill, L1 capacity could be exceeded until this situation is over (concerning inter PRB pooling algorithm, some desynchronizations between L1 and L2 may also occur).

[CNI-127661]

If, due to an overload situation and skip of slots, the above processing cannot be fulfilled, L1 capacity could be exceeded for UE pooling until this situation is over. For PRB pooling, the scheduler uses the min values until this situation is over, in such way that the L1 capacity should not be exceeded. 

[End CNI-127661].

#### 6.8.4.1.0-2 Common Trigger for the periodic Allocation of the pooled Resources (ID: 11246598)

**6.8.4.1.0-2.0-2**  (ID: `11246612`)

IF( (lastAllocationSfn == invalidSfn) OR (ResourceReq.sfn + bbPoolEvalPeriod >= lastAllocationSfn) )
