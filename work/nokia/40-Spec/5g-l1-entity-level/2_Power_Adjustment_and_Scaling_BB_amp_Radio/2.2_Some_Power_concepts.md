# 2.2 Some Power concepts

## 2.2.1 Definitons of Power parameters

**2.2.1.0-1**  (ID: `11415987`)

Definitons of Power parameters
Refer to [11118320: Power Per Component Carrier](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kqB9PfvSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ) (CNI-46722 onwards), there are several power related definitions for better understanding the relationship.

**Pmax: Maximum output power per TRX in dBm.**

• Generally, one PA per TRX, therefore Pmax = PA power per TRX

• Pcc = Pmax + 10log10(Num. of TRXs per RU); // where Num. of TRXs per RU = 2 x Num. of TRXs per polar

• Practically, NIDD setting of pMax is important, it makes sure that the applied power will NOT exceed the maximum output power allowed by the RU

• According to HW RU module specification, the setting of pMax shall always give the value lower than maximum output power according to the number of TRXs per RU, meaning that:

• NIDD: pMax (dBm) <= Max output power – 10log10(Num. of TRXs per RU)

**powerBudgetPerBeam: = Pmax + 10log10(Num. of TRXs per polar); //Total power per polarization or total power per TRX array or it is the power budget of all streams per polar**

NOTE: It is defined in 5G\_L3\_IF\_1153, where it can be understood that the "Beam" denotes the "Polar Beam" for TDD case.

**powerBudgetPerStream: = Pmax + 10log10(Num. of TRXs per polar) – 10log10(Num. of streams per polar); //Power per stream per polarization**

Relative dBFs
A power reduction for a given physical channel or signal, expressed in “relative dBFS”, is the difference in dB between the transmitted power per spatial stream for one RE of this channel or signal and the total transmitted power per beam polarization (**powerBudgetPerBeam, for the sake of simple description, here use Ptot as abbreviation in later section [11416015](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_e-ScOPvvEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)) of the OFDM symbol to which this RE belongs. This total power per beam polarization of the OFDM symbol itself should in turn be at a back-off value B (dB) below Full Scale (FS), as illustrated in below figure (refer to [11118673: CPRI-based Power Scaling (Beamforming-type)](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kqEZfPvSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)).** Given this definition, power reductions in dBFS are always expressed by negative numbers. however B is taken as positive in section [11416015](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_e-ScOPvvEe-AqvopbP1qhQ).\*\*\*\*

NOTE: Generally, L1 assume -18.2 per stream for DL MIMO with beamforming case

• If there are 2 C2 with 1 C2 per polarization, then there are one stream per polarization, therefore corresponding to total of -18.2.

• If there are C4 with 2-stream per polarization (2 times -18.2), then -3dB (under power control of L2PS) is required to guarantee to the total output of -18.2.

The same rule is applied to L1 eCPRI case without digital back-off, that’s to say, the maximum eCPRI signal level sum of RE power in frequency domain per stream is corresponding to total of 0 dBFs.

## 2.2.3 Other Definitions

**2.2.3.0-1**  (ID: `11416012`)

Other Definitions
**Logical antenna port: a logical antenna port corresponds to a layer, before precoding.**

**“Physical” antenna port: for the transmitter, a “physical” antenna port corresponds to one of the outputs of the precoding operation. The reason why “physical” is put between quotes is that this corresponds to a real physical antenna port only in the case where no RF beamforming is applied. When RF beamforming is applied, the outputs of the precoding operation are not real physical antenna ports, but beam polarizations.**

For the receiver, a “physical” antenna port also corresponds to a real physical antenna port when no RF beamforming is applied and to a beam polarization when RF beamforming is applied.

Above definitions shall be applied in the whole section [11415882](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_e-ROHPvvEe-AqvopbP1qhQ).
