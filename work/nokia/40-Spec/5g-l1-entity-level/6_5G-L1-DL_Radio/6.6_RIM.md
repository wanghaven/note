"For eCPRI scenario, the “IFFT  RIM-RS CP addition  Normal CP removal  FFT” steps in Figure 3‑14 could be simplified as an application of a single phase shift on “symbol 0” FD signal. It’s clear in Figure 3‑15 that, the original TD RIM-RS signal has same content “Seq\_1” on “symbol 0” and “symbol 1”. After rearrangement, the signal on “symbol 0” (blue range) becomes to “Seq\_2" while the signal on “symbol 1” (yellow range) is still “Seq\_1". ### 6.6.5.5 RIM-RS Multiplexing

#### 6.6.5.5.0-1 RIM-RS Multiplexing For RIM-RS transmission,  no multiplexing with other channels/signals is supported on same time-frequency resource. Possible conflict should be avoided from upper layer by omitting(for CSI-RS) or discarding (for PDSCH) other channels. No PDSCH on the slot RIM-RS scheduled  within whole BWP. No CSI-RS on the symbols  RIM-RS scheduled  within whole BWP. (ID: 11468864)

## 6.6.6 Capacity

### 6.6.6.0-1 Capacity RIM-RS capacity stands for the maximum number of RIM-RS resources per slot. One RIM-RS resource shall be mapped on two dedicated symbols configured for RIM-RS transmission. Currently (25R2 onwards), only one RIM-RS resource per cell per slot is supported. (ID: 11468873)
