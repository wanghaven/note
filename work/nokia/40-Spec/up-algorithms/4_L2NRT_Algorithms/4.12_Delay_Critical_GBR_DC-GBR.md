# 4.12 Delay Critical GBR (DC-GBR)

## 4.12.0-2 Delay Critical GBR (DC-GBR) Overview (ID: 11225008)

**4.12.0-2.0-2**  (ID: `13254060`)

Delay Critical GBR (DC-GBR)
gNB supports DC-GBR services as defined in 3GPP TS 23.501.

* Compared to GBR QoS flows, DC-GBR QoS flows are associated with an additional QoS parameter Maximum Data Burst Volume (MDBV). MDBV denotes the largest amount of data that the gNB is required to serve within a period of RAN\_PDB.
* PDB (Packet Delay Budget) is more stringent for DC-GBR than for GBR. PDB relationship with PER (Packet Error Rate) is also different than for GBR. For DC-GBR QoS Flows, a packet delayed more than PDB is counted as lost and included in the PER unless the data burst is exceeding the MDBV within the period of PDB or the QoS Flow is exceeding the GFBR.
* gNB supports DC-GBR 5QI values in the range [82...88]. In addition, Operator defined 5QI values in the range [128..254] can also be supported.
* [CB013365-A] The gNB shall support new DC-GBR 5QI values 89 and 90, as well as spare DC-GBR 5QI values in the ranges [11-64], 68, [77-78], 81, and [91-127] when the feature CB013365 is activated via operator configuration.

[End CB013365-A]

**DRB Configuration:**

* Single DC-GBR QoS flow per UE
* Each DC-GBR QoS flow mapped to a dedicated DRB (one-to-one mapping).
* Only RLC-UM mode supported.
* PDCP out-of-order delivery can be enabled or disabled.

**Mobility:**

* Intra-NR mobility is supported for DC-GBR.
* Inter-RAT mobility to LTE is supported.

## 4.12.0-3 Delay Critical GBR (DC-GBR) Prioritization of DC-GBR traffic over GBR and non-GBR traffic (ID: 11225021)

**4.12.0-3.0-1**  (ID: `11225027`)

Delay Critical GBR (DC-GBR)
\* gNB-CU-UP and gNB-DU shall prioritize DC-GBR QoS traffic over other GBR and non-GBR QoS traffic.

Note: The realization of the prioritization of DC-GBR traffic packets over other services is left to implementation.

* gNB-CU-UP and gNB-DU shall prioritize servicing of the DC-GBR services over others during buffer congestion situations as defined in 5G\_UP\_ALG\_7928\_replaced([11224070](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kyFF-fvSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)) and buffer overflow situations as defined in 5G\_UP\_ALG\_25799\_replaced([11224100](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kyFF_fvSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)).

## 4.12.0-4 Delay Critical GBR (DC-GBR) Flow Control for DC-GBR (ID: 11225032)

**4.12.0-4.0-1**  (ID: `11225040`)

Delay Critical GBR (DC-GBR)
\* gNB-CU-UP shall handle the DC-GBR as follows, all existing GBR related requirements are applicable for DC-GBR unless specified otherwise :
\* Computation of estimated throughput shall be performed as defined in 5G\_UP\_ALG\_5486\_replaced([11219236](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kxpBGvvSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)).
\* For each incoming DC-GBR PDCP SDU, the flow control algorithm shall ensure compliance to the Maximum Data Burst Volume (MDBV) configured, and shall process the SDU as defined in the Sliding Window Algorithm in 5G\_UP\_ALG\_30921\_replaced([11225048](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kyLMk_vSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)).
\* The DC-GBR PDCP PDU shall be transmitted as defined in 5G\_UP\_ALG\_5630\_replaced([11220250](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kxvHtPvSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)).
\* PDCP SDUs shall be discarded based on the configured discard timer value as specified in 5G\_UP\_ALG\_7930\_replaced([11224150](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kyFtBfvSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)).

* gNB-DU (L2-Hi DU) shall handle the DC-GBR bearers similar to GBR bearers and all GBR related requirements are applicable for DC-GBR unless specified otherwise. 5G\_UP\_ALG\_8433\_replaced([11221307](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kx0nTfvSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)), 5G\_UP\_ALG\_5801\_replaced([11221973](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kx44svvSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)) can be referred.
* gNB-DU shall support DC-GBR in RLC UM mode only and hence all RLC UM requirements are applicable as is to DC-GBR unless specified otherwise.

**4.12.0-5.0-2**  (ID: `11225072`)

Delay Critical GBR (DC-GBR)
Definitions

---

**4.12.0-5.0-2.0-1**  (ID: `11225079`)

Delay Critical GBR (DC-GBR)
\* consumption\_in\_current\_window (bytes): Indicates the total number of bytes sent in the current sliding window
\* window\_capacity (bytes): Indicates the maximum number of bytes that can be sent in any sliding window. This is equal to `MDBV * (1 + dlMdbvMargin)`
\* window\_size (in ms): Indicates the size of the time window where the MDBV is applied. This is equal to RAN PDB configuration.
\* time\_of\_arrival (timestamp): Captures the timestamp at the time of arrival of a particular PDCP SDU.

**4.12.0-5.0-3**  (ID: `11225083`)

Delay Critical GBR (DC-GBR)
Initialization

---

**4.12.0-5.0-3.0-1**  (ID: `11225090`)

Delay Critical GBR (DC-GBR)
\* consumption\_in\_current\_window = 0 (in bytes)
\* window\_capacity = MDBV \* (1 + dlMdbvMargin) (in bytes)
\* window\_size = RAN\_PDB (in ms)
\* Initialize MEMORY BUFFER

Note: dlMdbvMargin is a NIDD parameter that can be configured. It will have a range between 0 to 1 in steps of 0.1.

**4.12.0-5.0-4**  (ID: `11225097`)

Delay Critical GBR (DC-GBR)
Calculation of consumption\_in\_current\_window

---

**4.12.0-5.0-4.0-1**  (ID: `11225105`)

Delay Critical GBR (DC-GBR)
\* current\_window = current\_time - window\_size
\* consumption\_in\_current\_window = Sum of the size of all the PDCP SDU(s) with in the sliding window i.e, arrival time of the PDCP SDU is within the current\_window
\* gNB-CU-UP shall release the reference to each of the PDCP SDU(s) in the MEMORY BUFFER which are outside of the current sliding window.

**4.12.0-5.0-5**  (ID: `11225111`)

Delay Critical GBR (DC-GBR)
Handling of DC-GBR PDCP SDU on arrival

---

**4.12.0-5.0-5.0-1**  (ID: `11225116`)

Delay Critical GBR (DC-GBR)
To reduce the delay of waiting till the next period, gNB-CU-UP shall check the capacity on the arrival of each DC-GBR PDCP SDU and process immediately if there is sufficient capacity

• time\_of\_arrival = current\_time

• Calculate consumption\_in\_current\_window as defined in 5G\_UP\_ALG\_30928\_replaced([11225097](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kyLMmvvSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ))

IF (consumption\_in\_current\_window  + size\_of(PDCP SDU) <= window\_capacity): // DC-GBR PDCP SDU can be accommodated within MDBV

• consumption\_in\_current\_window  = consumption\_in\_current\_window  + size\_of(DC-GBR PDCP SDU)

• Store the size\_of(DC-GBR PDCP SDU) and the time\_of\_arrival in the MEMORY BUFFER

• Process DC-GBR PDCP SDU to PDCP PDU as defined in 5G\_UP\_ALG\_5628\_replaced([11220228](https://dn-prod.ext.net.nokia.com/rm/resources/BI_kxvHsfvSEe-AqvopbP1qhQ)).

ELSE

• Discard DC-GBR PDCP SDU   // due to MDBV limit exceeded

## 4.12.0-7 Delay Critical GBR (DC-GBR) GFBR and MDBV Compliance assignment (ID: 11225156)

**4.12.0-7.0-1**  (ID: `11225161`)

Delay Critical GBR (DC-GBR)
Missing UL PDCP PDUs in Uplink:

* For missing PDCP PDUs in Uplink (determined at PDCP t-reordering timer expiry), gNB-CU-UP shall use the following rule to determine if the missing PDCP PDUs are GFBR and MDBV compliant or not:
* If the PDCP PDUs on both ends of the missing sequence are GFBR and MDBV compliant, then the missing PDUs are considered to be compliant.
* If the PDCP PDUs on either or both ends are GFBR/MDBV non-compliant, then the missing PDCP PDUs are considered to be non-compliant.

Example**: Consider the following scenario in UL PDCP**

* PDCP SNs 1,2,3 and 5 are successfully received at UL. PDCP t-reordering timer is started on reception of PDCP PDU with SN 5.
* On PDCP t-reordering timer expiry, missing PDCP PDU with SN 4 is considered compliant if both PDCP PDUs with SN 3 and 5 are compliant. If either or both of PDCP PDUs with SN 3 and 5 are GFBR non-compliant, then PDCP PDU with SN 4 is considered non-compliant.

[PR841971 - 25R3]

UL MDBV Compliance check for large UL PDCP SDUs:

* gNB-CU-UP shall mark the UL PDCP SDUs as UL MDBV non-compliant if the size of the UL PDCP SDU exceeds the configured UL MDBV value (refer NIDD parameter NRBTS.NRDRB.delayCriticalGbrDrbParam.ulMdbv).

[End PR841971 - 25R3]

**Note: Only GFBR and MDBV compliant PDCP PDUs are considered for updating UL Lost PDCP SDU counters for UL PER calculation.**
